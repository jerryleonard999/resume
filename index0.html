Career Summary

I have 20+ years of hands-on experience in the semiconductor and optics industries as a Process Development Engineer, Sustaining Engineer, Applications Engineer, Research Scientist and Program Manager.

I have developed and optimized processes for applications including micro-electronic, micro-mechanical, micro-optical and Diffractive Optical Element devices (computer generated holograms). This experience includes work supporting high-volume silicon production lines as well as research and development on novel substrates, including gallium arsenide and mercury cadmium telluride (IR focal plane arrays). I have also developed, optimized and patented processes for silicon-based micro-electro-mechanical (MEMs) devices. In addition to extensive hands-on experience with numerous micro-fabrication techniques, I have decades of expertise in taking complex and innovative projects from conception to prototype production as a process engineer and program manager.

Fabrication expertise includes photolithography (both thick-film photoresist & high-resolution photoresist, from DUV and i-line scanners and steppers (ASML and Nikon) to i-line contact printing), electron microscopy, metal deposition processes (Au-plating, thermal and e-beam evaporation), and metal lift-off processing.
